# Products & Services Script

**Intro – Overview**  
Welcome to the OneCX platform — your centralized solution for seamless customer engagement. In this video, we'll guide you through the Product Categories and Products/Services modules, essential tools for organizing and managing the offerings visible to your customers through the OneCX platform.

**Product Categories**

1. **Accessing the Product Category Module**

To access the 'Product Categories’ module, navigate to the ‘Locations & Services’ section and click on ‘Product Categories’.   
 You will be directed to a grid that displays all configured product categories.

2. **Product Categories Grid Overview**

The grid view provides a high-level summary of all created product categories. Columns include:

* Category Name  
* Category Display Name  
* Category Code  
* Category Icon  
* Show in Widget  
* Action – include the ‘Edit’ and ‘Delete’ icons.

Filter options are available by language which supports English and Spanish. You can also use the search bar to filter locations. 

3. **Configuring a Product Category**

Click the ‘Add Product Category’ button to create a new one or use the edit icon to modify an existing category.  
Fill product category information which includes the discussed fields such as:

* **Language:** The language for the category.  
* **Category Name:** The internal name for your category.  
* **Category Display Name:** The category name will appear to your customers.  
* **Category Code:** A unique identifier for the category.  
* **Category Icon:** An icon that visually represents the category.  
* **Show in Widget:** Controls if you want this category to be visible in customer-facing widgets, such as the Kiosk or the Appointment Web Widget.

Once you've entered all the necessary information, click ‘Save’. Your new category will now appear in the grid view. Clicking on ‘Cancel’ will discard the changes.

4. **Setting up the display order of product categories**

To set the display order of your categories \- click on the ‘Set Display Order’ hyperlink at the top right of the grid.  
The product categories will be displayed in the order of the product category code by default.  
To customize the display order, you can simply drag and drop the categories into your desired sequence. Once satisfied, click ‘Save’. 

This order will be reflected in areas where categories are displayed, such as the Kiosk and Appointments Widget if the 'Show Categories' option was enabled.

**Products & Services**

5. **Accessing the Products & Services Module**

To access the 'Products & Services’ module, navigate to the ‘Locations & Services’ section and click on ‘Products & Services’.   
You will be directed to a grid that displays all configured products and services.

6. **Products & Services Grid Overview**

The grid view provides a high-level summary of all products and services created. Columns include:

* **Service Name:** The internal name for the product or service.   
* **Service Display Name:** The product or service name which will appear to customers.   
* **Service Code:** A unique identifier.   
* **Category:** The category assigned to the product or service.   
* **Type:** The type specifies whether it's a ‘Service’ or a ‘Product’.  
* **Action**: Include the ‘Edit’, ‘Deactivate/Activate’ and ‘Delete’ icons.

Filter options are available by product category, type, active status, and language. You can also use the search bar to filter locations.   
Click the ‘Add Product or Service’ button to create a new one or use the edit icon to modify an existing service.

7. **Configuring a Product or Service**

The products and services add/edit screen uses a task-based UI organized into multiple collapsible sections:

* Basic Settings  
* Opt in Settings  
* Employee Settings  
* Appointment Settings  
* Lobby Settings" sections

We’ll walk through each section in detail below.

### **Basic Settings Section**  

This captures the core identification and configuration values required to set up a product/service within OneCX. In the ‘Basic Settings’ section, you'll configure details such as:

* **Language:** The language for this product or service.  
* **Service Name:** The internal name for the product or service.  
* **Service Display Name:** How the product or service will appear to customers.  
* **Service Code:** A unique identifier.  
* **Category:** Assign the product or service to a previously created category.  
* **Type:** Specify whether it's a "Service" or a "Product".  
* **Active:** Mark as active to make it available.  
* **Description:** An optional field for internal notes.

When saving a new product/service for the first time, we have two options as ‘Save’ and ‘Save and Continue’

* Clicking on ‘Save’ navigates back to the products/services grid. The product/service appears in the main grid.  
* Clicking on ‘Save and Continue’ retains the same view allowing you to edit other settings after saving the product/service.

When closing the section, click “Close” to minimize the expanded section  

**Opt In Settings Section**    
Allows you to control customer opt-ins for this specific product or service. The grid columns include:

* **Location:** Displays all active branch location names.  
* **Parent:** The associated parent location of the related branches.   
* **Opt In:** Here, you can configure opt-in preferences on a per-location basis for the related product or service.

Filter options are available by Opt In Status and Location.

**Employee Settings Section**    
Allows you to manage which employees are proficient in offering this particular product or service. The grid columns include:

* **First Name** and **Last Name** of active employees.  
* **Home Location:** The associated home location of the related employees.  
* **Opt In:** Here, you can configure opt-in preferences on a per-employee basis for the related product or service.  
* **Proficiency:** Allows to determine the skill proficiency range between 0 – 100 for an employee to provide that product/service.

Filter options are available by Opt In Status and Location.

**Appointment Settings Section**    
This is crucial for managing how this product or service integrates with the appointment booking system. The grid columns include:

* Location Name  
* Parent  
* Duration  
* Show in Widget  
* Meeting Preferences such as In Person, Phone, Virtual  
* Restrict Modify  
* Require Comments  
* Action – includes the ‘Edit’ icon

Filter options are available by Location.

By clicking on the ‘Edit’ icon of a location record in the ‘Action’ column, an ‘Edit Appointment Settings’ pop-up will appear.

* Within this pop-up, under ‘General Settings’, you can customize various basic appointment parameters for this product or service:  
  * **Duration:** Define the default duration of appointments for this service. For example, appointments are typically set for 30 minutes.  
  * **Show in Widget:** Determine if this service is available for appointment booking through the customer-facing widget.  
  * **Meeting Preferences:** Specify available meeting types, such as In-Person, Phone, or Virtual appointments.   
  * **Restrict Modify/Cancel:** You can restrict customers from modifying or canceling appointments for this service.  
  * **Require Comments:** Enable this option to mandate customers to provide comments when booking appointments for this service.  
* Under the ‘Appointment Checklist’ tab, you can add or modify checklists that will be reflected in appointment confirmation, reminder, and update emails.  
* And under the ‘Comments’ tab, you can configure settings related to customer comments:  
  * You can customize the text displayed for comments in FMSI Appointments and the Widget. For example, it could be ‘Please provide additional details’.

The changes you make here are specific to the related branch for which the record was opened. However, when saving, you have the flexibility to apply customized changes across other accessible branches as well.

**Lobby Settings Section**    
Controls how the product or service appears and functions within the lobby and kiosks. The grid columns include:

* Location Name  
* Parent  
* Show in Check-In/Wrap Up  
* Show in Kiosk  
* Time Standard: Configure settings for specific locations.  
* Action – includes the ‘Edit’ icon

Filter options are available by Location.

By clicking on the ‘Edit’ icon of a location record in the ‘Action’ column, an ‘Edit Lobby Settings’ pop-up will appear.

* **Time Standard:** Set a time standard for this service within the lobby. For example, ‘Canton’ has a time standard of 30\.  
* **Show in Check-In/Wrap-up:** Determine if this product or service is visible during the check-in and wrap-up processes.  
* **Show in Kiosk:** Control whether this product or service is displayed on the Kiosk for customer selection.

The changes you make here are specific to the related branch for which the record was opened. However, when saving, you have the flexibility to apply customized changes across other accessible branches as well.

**Saving a Section** – Click on “Save” to update changes done in the Opt in Settings, Employee Settings, Appointment Settings, and Lobby Settings" sections

**Closing a Section** – Click “Close” to minimize the expanded section.

8. **Setting up the display order of products & services**

Similar to product categories, you can also set the display order for your products and services.   
To set the display order of your services \- click on the ‘Set Display Order’ hyperlink at the top right of the grid.  
The product and services will be displayed in the order of the product/service code by default. Here, you can define the "Global Display Order" or the "Category Display Order".   
To set a custom display order, simply drag and drop the products and services into your desired order.   
Once satisfied, click the ‘Save’ button. 

When saving you have the flexibility to apply this customized order to specific branches as well.

* If the related 'Show Category' option is unchecked under 'Global Settings’ and ‘Kiosk Configuration’, the products/services display order set under the ‘Global Display Order’ will be reflected in the Lobby Check-in, Lobby Wrap-Up, and Kiosk.  
* And if the 'Show Category' option is unchecked then the products/services display order set under the ‘Category Display Order’ will be reflected in the Lobby Check-in, Lobby Wrap-Up, Kiosk and Widget.    
* Additionally, if the 'Show Category' option is checked, it will also be reflected in the Widget.

This level of granular control ensures that each product or service is presented and managed precisely according to your operational needs across all OneCX touchpoints.

And that's a look at how the Product Categories and Products/Services modules work in OneCX. By effectively organizing your offerings, you enhance the customer journey, making it easier for them to navigate and select the services they need.
