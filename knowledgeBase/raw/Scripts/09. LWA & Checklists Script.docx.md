**Left Without Assistance Module**  
To access the ‘Left Without Assistance’ module, go to the left-hand menu panel, scroll to the ‘Configuration’ section, and click on the ‘Left Without Assistance’ option.

Unlike in Lobby Tracker, where ‘Left Without Assistance’ reasons are configured as products or services, here in OneCX, we’ve done it differently. We've created a separate module for maintaining ‘Left Without Assistance’ reasons. 

A grid will be available with two columns as ‘Reason’ and ‘Action’. By default, you’ll see a defined reason as ‘Left without Assistance’. This is added from the backend and will be available to all clients by default. However, they can be edited by clicking on the ‘Edit’ icon or deleted by clicking on the ‘Delete’ icon of the related reason record.

You can also add new custom reasons. To do that, click on the ‘Add Reason’ button and a new record will be populated in the grid to enter the new reason in the text field. Once the ‘Save’ icon is clicked, the reason gets added to the grid. To discard changes, click on the ‘Cross’ icon.

Now where do these reasons come into play?

They impact the Lobby module. Let’s switch over to the Lobby screen. Here, for each visitor record that’s currently checked into the Lobby, you’ll see a Left Without Assistance option. When clicked, the list of all reasons from the ‘Left Without Assistance’ module will appear here as options. Users can also enter an additional comment if needed and submit it.

So that wraps up the ‘Left Without Assistance’ module. Its main point of impact is the ‘Lobby’ module and eventually in the reporting, which will be covered in a separate KT.

**Checklists Module**  
Next, let’s move on to the ‘Checklists’ module.

To access this, go to the left-hand panel, open the ‘Locations & Services’ section, and then click on ‘Checklists’.

This feature functions similarly to how it worked in the Lobby Tracker. For every available product or service, you can define a checklist that staff members must complete during service wrap-up.

You can use the ‘Product Category’ dropdown to filter the services. Based on the selected category, you’ll only see the related products/services.

A grid will be available with four columns as ‘Products/Service’, ‘Product Category’, ‘Number of Checklist Items’ and ‘Action’. 

**Creating, Editing or Deleting a Checklist** 

* To configure a checklist for a product/service for the first time, you can click on the ‘Plus’ icon in the ‘Action’ column of the related product/service record. You will navigate to the ‘Add Checklist’ page.  
* To view or modify an existing checklist configured for a product/service, you can click the ‘Edit’ icon in the ‘Action’ column of the related product/service record. You will navigate to the ‘Edit Checklist’ page.  
* To delete an existing checklist configured for a product/service, you can click the 'Delete’ icon in the ‘Action’ column of the related product/service record.

Whether you're creating a new checklist or editing an existing one, the layout is the same.

Each checklist item will have:

* A name used for internal reference purposes – which is the ‘Checklist Item’.   
* A name used reflected across the application where the checklists are used \- which is the ‘Display Text’.

**Creating, Editing or Deleting a Checklist Item**     
To configure a checklist item for the first time, you can click on the ‘Add Checklist’ button in the ‘Action’ column, and a new checklist item record will be populated in the grid to enter the required information. The checklist item can be saved or discarded using the ‘Save’ or ‘Cancel’ icons in the ‘Action’ column.

To modify an existing checklist item configured within a checklist, you can click the ‘Edit’ icon in the ‘Action’ column of the related checklist item and record in the grid will become editable. Any changes can be saved or discarded using the ‘Save’ or ‘Cancel’ icons in the ‘Action’ column.  
To delete an existing checklist item configured within a checklist, you can click the 'Delete’ icon in the ‘Action’ column of the related checklist item record.

You can also reorder checklist items by clicking and dragging the reorder icon. The order you define here will be reflected in the ‘Wrap Up’ pop up which can be accessed through the ‘Lobby’ module or by clicking on the ‘Wrap Up’ button available in the bottom of the left menu panel.

**Where are checklists reflected?**   
These checklists appear when the staff member wraps up a visit in the Lobby module. For products and services that have checklists defined, a checklist icon will appear next to the product or service line item in the ‘Wrap Up’ view. 

Before completing the service, all checklist items must be checked off by the staff member to indicate that the customer has submitted / bought the relevant artifacts required to obtain the service. Only then will the service be marked as completed.

If no checklist is defined for the service, the wrap-up process proceeds without that step.

And that’s a quick walkthrough of the 'Left Without Assistance’ and ‘Checklist modules in OneCX. These tools are designed to give you complete control over how your system is configured and presented to users.
