# Lobby Script

**Intro – Overview**  
Welcome to the OneCX platform — your centralized solution for seamless customer engagement. In this video, we’ll walk through the Lobby module designed to help you manage the sign-in flow, visitor check-in, wrap-up, staff views, central views, and force sign-out behavior.

# **1\. Sign-In Flow**

When you log into the application, you are immediately presented with the ‘Sign-In’ pop-up. This pop-up displays a hierarchical list of locations where the user is eligible to sign in — including both home and float locations.

* You can select a branch and click ‘Sign’ In to begin.  
* To sign out, click the “Sign Out of Lobby” button at the top right of the header panel. The same button can be used to sign in again.

# **2\. KPI Summary**

At the top of the Lobby module, KPIs are displayed:

* Visitors Waiting  
* Average Wait Time  
* Staff Signed In  
* Visitors Served

These statistics are location-specific, determined by the branch selected in the Lobby Status or Appointment Status dropdowns. Even if you're signed out, selecting a location will refresh the KPIs and grid data accordingly.

# **3\. Changing Signed-In Location**

OneCX doesn’t have a main location selector. Instead, changing the location in the Lobby or Appointment Status dropdown will also update your signed-in location. The ‘Signed In/Out Location’ dropdowns for Lobby and Appointment views are synced — changes in one reflect in the other.

# **4\. Float Locations**

If a user has float locations configured, they can:

* View appointment requests from float branches.  
* Assist float-location visitors through the Lobby.

Float locations are visible in the ‘Lobby Queue’ and ‘Appointment Queue’ filters and on the ‘Home’ dashboard.

# **5\. Visitor Check-In Behavior**

You can check in a visitor even without being signed in to the Lobby. The ‘Check-In’ button is located at the bottom left of the application regardless of whichever module you are in the application.

Visitor Info Includes:

* Account Number (optional)  
* First Name and Last Name (mandatory)  
* Phone Number  
* Receive Text Message checkbox  
* Notes (non-sensitive info only)  
* Products & Services (grouped by category if setting enabled)  
* Staff Preference (based on signed-in users & their notification groups)  
* Spanish Speaker Preference

The visibility of Account Number, Phone Number, Email Address, Date of Birth and Custom fields can be controlled through the ‘Global Settings’ module. You can use the:

* ‘Today’s Appointments’ button to access the ‘Appointments Queue’ of the signed in branch.  
* ‘Add to Lobby’ button to add records directly to the Lobby.   
* ‘Instance Assist’ button will navigate you to the ‘Wrap Up’ view to start assisting the visitor.   
* ‘Instant Assist (Impersonate)’ button will support assisting a visitor by impersonating another staff member. You can view more details in the ‘Impersonation’ training video.

# **6\. Lobby Queue**

Each row in the grid displays:

* Type – Indicates the source or nature of the record (e.g., walk-in, appointment)  
* Location – Branch where the visitor is currently checked in  
* Account Number  
* First Name / Last Name  
* Staff Requested  
* Requested Services  
* Wait Time  
* Action Icons – Includes Assist, Impersonate Assist, Edit, Left without Assistance and Text on Demand.

# **7\. Visitor Wrap-Up Behavior**

You can assist a visitor by clicking on the ‘Assist / Impersonate Assist’ icons in the lobby grid. Once the assist begins, if you close the ‘Wrap Up’ view, a ‘Wrap Up’ button will be displayed along with the ‘Check-In’ button to access from anywhere in the application. The ‘Wrap Up’ view consists of:

* The visitor details including the requested services.  
* Requested or new services can be selected to add them to the ‘Completed Requests’ section. For services with checklists:  
  * A checklist icon appears.  
  * Clicking it displays checklist items, including hyperlinks.  
* Other features include the ability to:  
  * View & edit service reference numbers  
  * View running interaction time and assisted by details.  
    * Every time a record is returned to the lobby and reassisted, it counts as one interaction. Each visitor record can have upto 5 interactions.  
    * On the 5th (final) interaction, the ‘Return to Lobby’ button will be disabled, and the staff must wrap up the record.  
  * Add required comments and wrap up to complete visitor assistance, book a follow up appointment or return the visitor to the lobby.

# **8\. Editing Lobby Record**

To edit or update visitor details or service requests, click the ‘Edit’ icon on the relevant checked-in lobby record. All information entered during the check-in process can be modified from here.

# **9\. Left Without Assistance**

You can mark a lobby record as ‘Left Without Assistance’ by clicking on the ‘Left Without Assistance’ icon in the lobby grid. Once clicked, a pop up will be displayed with a list of predefined reasons (e.g., Excessive Wait Time).  
Users can select a reason and optionally enter a description. Once saved, the record will be removed from the queue.

**10\. Text on Demand**  
To send ad-hoc text messages to visitors who have opted in, click the ‘Text on Demand’ icon in the lobby grid. Enter your message in the pop-up and click ‘Send’ — the message will be delivered to the selected visitor immediately.

# **11\. Staff Status View**

Each row in this view displays:

* Type – Indicates staff role or status source  
* Location – Branch where the staff member is signed in  
* Staff Name  
* Status/Time – Whether they’re assisting or idle, along with duration  
* Currently Assisting – The name of the visitor currently being helped  
* Request(s) – Services being assisted  
* Current Assist Time  
* Action Icons – Includes information and force sign out.

Filter options are available by assist type.

# **12\. Interaction Details**

More details on the visitor and interaction (Added Time, Assist Start Time, Current Assist Time) can be viewed by clicking on the ‘Information’ icon in the 'Staff Status’ grid. 

# **13\. Force Sign Out**

To force sign out a user, click on the ‘Sign Out’ icon in the ‘Staff Status’ grid. 

* You cannot sign out yourself however you can sign out other idle users immediately.   
* If a user is assisting, a confirmation pop-up will appear.   
* Force sign-out is disabled when the related user is the last one signed in at the branch or if the assisting record is on its 5th interaction.

# **14\. Appointment Queue Tab**

The Appointment Queue tab displays all same-day appointments scheduled for the selected location. Each row provides the following details:

* Appointment Type – Indicates whether the appointment is in-person, phone, or virtual  
* Confirmation Number  
* Visitor Name  
* Requested Services  
* Requested Staff Member  
* Notes  
* Appointment Time  
* Current Status  
* The Action column includes icons that allow staff to:  
  * Check in the appointment into the lobby  
  * Edit appointment details  
  * Mark the appointment as a no-show

# **15\. Central Views**

To access the central views, click on the ‘Central Views’ tab within the Lobby module. This section is designed to provide a consolidated, cross-location overview of lobby activity, staff status, and operational metrics across all branches under the logged-in user's client.  
There are three sub-views available within the Central Views tab:

* #### **Central Lobby View**

This grid displays a combined list of all visitors currently checked into the lobby across every branch the user has access to. It functions similarly to the standard Lobby Queue view with the same layout and grid columns, but instead of being limited to the signed-in location, it aggregates records from all client branches. Filter options are available by location and Spanish speaker requests.

* **Central Staff Status View**

This section provides a real-time history of all staff members who are signed in across the client’s branches. It functions similarly to the standard Staff Status view with the same layout and grid columns and helps supervisors monitor availability and workload distribution. Filter options are available by location and assist type.

* #### **Central Location View**

This view displays performance metrics by location, helping users and administrators compare and evaluate service efficiency across branches.  
Each row includes:

* Location  
* Current Wait Time  
* Visitors Waiting in Lobby  
* Total Assists  
* Total Products & Services Provided  
* Average Assist Time  
* Average Wait Time  
* Total Abandons

Filter options are available by location.

And that’s a quick walkthrough of the Lobby module in OneCX. These tools are designed to bring together real-time visitor management, staff availability, and service tracking across all branches—ensuring an efficient and streamlined customer service experience.
